Khajuraho Temple

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments, located in Madhya Pradesh,
India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its
stunning temples built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela
dynasty.These temples are renowned for their intricate carvings and
erotic sculptures, which depict various aspects of life,
mythology,and spirituality. The architecture showcases a perfect
blend of Nagara-style design with elaborate spires and detailed
stonework. Of the original 85 temples, around 20 have survived,
including the notable Kandariya Mahadeva Temple. Khajuraho
remains a testament to medieval India's artistic and architectural
prowess,attracting visitors worldwide.
