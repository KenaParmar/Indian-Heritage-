Rani Ki Vav

Rani ki Vav, located in Patan, Gujarat, India, is an
intricately constructed stepwell built in the 11th century
by Queen Udayamati in memory of her husband, King Bhima
I of the Chalukya dynasty. This UNESCO World Heritage Site
features seven levels of stairs and over 500 principal sculptures,
showcasing exquisite craftsmanship in Maru-Gurjara
architecture. The stepwell was designed as an inverted temple,
highlighting the sanctity of water, and includes detailed
carvings depicting mythological and religious themes.Rani ki
Vav is a remarkable example of ancient engineering and artistry,
attracting visitors with its historical and architectural splendor.
