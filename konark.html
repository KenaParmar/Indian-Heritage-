Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple, located in Odisha, India,
is a 13th-century marvel built by King Narasimhadeva I
of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. Designed as a colossal
chariot of the Sun God, Surya, it features 24 intricately
carved stone wheels and is drawn by seven horses. The temple
is renowned for its exquisite Kalinga architecture,
detailed carvings, and symbolic representations of time and
celestial bodies. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Konark
Sun Temple stands as a testament to ancient Indian craftsmanship
and astronomical knowledge, attracting visitors for its
historical significance and architectural grandeur.
