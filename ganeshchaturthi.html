Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi, also known as Vinayaka Chaturthi,
is a popular Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord
Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity of wisdom and prosperity.
Celebrated with great enthusiasm in late August or early September,
the festival lasts for 10 days. It begins with the installation of
Ganesha idols in homes and public pandals (temporary stages),
followed by daily prayers, chanting, and offerings of sweets
like modaks, Ganesha's favorite. The festival culminates in a grand
procession and the immersion (visarjan) of the idols in water,
symbolizing Ganesha's return to his celestial abode. Ganesh Chaturthi
fosters community spirit and cultural richness, bringing people
together in joyous celebration and devotion.
