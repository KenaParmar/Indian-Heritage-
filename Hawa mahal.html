Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, located in Jaipur, India, is a unique five-story
palace built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Made of red
and pink sandstone, it is known as the "Palace of Winds" due to its
953 small windows, or jharokhas, which allow cool breezes to flow
through, keeping the palace comfortable in the heat.
The intricate latticework and honeycomb design reflect the fine
craftsmanship of the Rajput architecture. Originally constructed
for the royal ladies to observe street festivals
without being seen, Hawa Mahal remains a prominent
tourist attraction, showcasing Jaipur's
rich cultural heritage.
