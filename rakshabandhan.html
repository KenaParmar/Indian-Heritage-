Rakshabandhan

Rakshabandhan, often referred to as Rakhi, is a traditional Hindu
festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters. On this
day, sisters tie a decorative thread called a rakhi around their
brothers' wrists, symbolizing love and protection. In return,
brothers give gifts to their sisters and promise to protect and
support them throughout their lives. The festival is marked by
family gatherings, the exchange of sweets, and rituals that
strengthen familial ties. Rakshabandhan fosters love, care, and
respect among siblings and reinforces the importance of family
relationships in Indian culture.
