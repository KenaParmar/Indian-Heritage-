Makarsankranti

Makar Sankranti, celebrated on January 14th each year,
is a major Hindu festival marking the transition of the Sun
into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara). This festival signifies
the end of the winter solstice and the beginning of longer
days. Known for its diverse regional names and customs
across India, it includes activities like kite flying, bonfires,
and the preparation of traditional sweets made from sesame
and jaggery. Makar Sankranti also has significant agricultural
importance, as it celebrates the harvest season, bringing
communities together in joy and thanksgiving for nature's bounty.
