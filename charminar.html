Charminar

The Charminar, located in Hyderabad, India, is an iconic
monument and mosque built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah.
This stunning structure features four granda rches facing
cardinal directions and four minarets, each standing
at 56 meters. Constructed in Indo-Islamic architecture
with Persian influences, the Charminar serves as a
symbol of Hyderabad. The monument, surrounded
by bustling markets, was originally constructed to
commemorate the end of a plague epidemic. Its strategic location
in the old city makes it a cultural and historical hub,attracting
numerous visitors for its architectural beauty and vibrant surroundings.
