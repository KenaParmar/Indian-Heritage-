Pongal

Pongal, celebrated primarily in Tamil Nadu, India, is a major
four-day harvest festival held in mid-January.It marks
the sun's northward journey (Uttarayan) and honors the Sun
God for a bountiful harvest. The festival's name is derived
from the traditional dish "Pongal," made from newly harvested
rice and sweetened milk. Each day of the festival has its
significance:Bhogi, Surya Pongal, Mattu Pongal, and Kanum Pongal.
Celebrations include decorating homes with kolam (rangoli), cooking
the Pongal dish, and offering prayers. Pongal fosters community
spirit and gratitude for agricultural prosperity, making it
a joyous and culturally rich festival.
